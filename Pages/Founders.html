Studio Ghibli Inc. is a Japanese animation film studio headquartered in Koganei, Tokyo. It is best known for its animated feature films and has also produced several short subjects, television commercials and two television films. Its mascot and most recognizable symbol is a character named Totoro, a giant catlike spirit from the 1988 anime film My Neighbor Totoro. The studio was founded on June 15, 1985 by directors Hayao Miyazaki, Isao Takahata and producer Toshio Suzuki, after the successful performance of Topcraft's Nausicaä of the Valley of the Wind (1984). It has also collaborated with video game studios on the visual development of several games.
Hayao Miyazaki

Hayao Miyazaki (born January 5, 1941) is a Japanese animator, director, producer, screenwriter, author, and manga artist. He has attained international acclaim as a masterful storyteller and creator of Japanese animated feature films, and is widely regarded as one of the most accomplished filmmakers in the history of animation.

He co-founded Studio Ghibli in 1985 and directed numerous films with Ghibli, including Castle in the Sky (1986), My Neighbor Totoro (1988), Kiki's Delivery Service (1989), and Porco Rosso (1992). The films were met with critical and commercial success in Japan.

Toshio Suzuki

Toshio Suzuki (born August 19, 1948) is a Japanese film producer of anime and a long-time colleague of Hayao Miyazaki, as well as the former president of Studio Ghibli. He is renowned as one of Japan's most successful producers after the enormous box office success (in Japan) of many Ghibli films.

In 1986 Suzuki served on the production committee for the Studio Ghibli film Laputa: Castle in the Sky for Tokuma Shoten and he succeeded Ogata as Animage editor-in-chief, also served on Tokuma Shoten's production committee for the Ghibli films My Neighbor Totoro, directed by Miyazaki, and Grave of the Fireflies directed by Takahata. Suzuki was associate producer on Kiki's Delivery Service and officially joined the studio as producer in 1989. In 1990 Suzuki was appointed director of the Studio Ghibli company.

Isao Takahata

Isao Takahata (October 29, 1935 April 5, 2018) was a Japanese director, screenwriter and producer. A co-founder of Studio Ghibli, he has earned international critical acclaim for his work as a director of Japanese animated feature films.

Takahata, Miyazaki and others formed Studio Ghibli in 1985, where he would direct Grave of the Fireflies (1988), Only Yesterday (1991), Pom Poko (1994), and My Neighbors the Yamadas (1999). His last film as director was The Tale of the Princess Kaguya (2013), which was nominated for an Oscar in the category of Best Animated Feature Film at the 87th Academy Awards.
